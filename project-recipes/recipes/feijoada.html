Feijoada

Description

Feijoada is a rich, hearty black bean stew that is considered the soul food of Brazil. Traditionally a slow-cooked feast for weekends and gatherings, this simplified version brings the deep, smoky flavors of the classic dish to your kitchen. It's a social meal meant to be shared with good company.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups black beans, dried
  • 200g smoked bacon, diced
  • 300g linguiça calabresa sausage, sliced
  • 300g pork ribs, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • To server: white rice, sautéed collard greens, orange slices and farofa

Steps

  1. Soak the black beans in water overnight.
  2. The next day, in a large heavy pot, brown the bacon, sausage, and pork ribs.
  3. Remove the meat and set aside.
  4. In the same pot, sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant.
  5. Add the drained beans, bay leaves, browned meat, and cover with water.
  6. Simmer for 2-3 hours, or until the beans and meat are tender, adding water as needed.
  7. Season with salt and pepper.
  8. Serve with white rice, collard greens, fresh orange slices, and a sprinkle of farofa.
